About CORWADO

Empowering rural women through sustainable agribusiness development since 2010

About Us

The Consortium of Rural Women for Agribusiness Development Organization (CORWADO) is a women-led, non-profit, national non-governmental organization (NNGO) based in South Sudan which share similar challenges, inspirations, values as well as a common destiny and as a vehicle to bring forth meaningful change both among the membership as well as the wider South Sudanese society bedevilled by years of conflict, marginalization and a host of vicious emergencies and resilience.

Our Vision

We envision a society that's free from poverty and thriving rural economies in South Sudan with access to services, markets and livelihoods for all.

Cultivate a future where every young person in South Sudan has the opportunity and resources to thrive and contribute positively to society and rural women are empowered, self-reliant, and leading sustainable development through inclusive agribusiness and community resilience.

Our Mission

To empower rural women and girls in South Sudan through sustainable agribusiness, economic empowerment, capacity building, and community-driven development, promoting gender equality, food security, and social transformation.

To empower rural women through sustainable agribusiness and entrepreneurship, enabling self-reliance, food security, and inclusive economic growth in South Sudan.

Broader Objectives

1. Promote Women's Economic Empowerment

  • Provide access to agribusiness skills training, market linkages, and microfinance.
  • Support the formation and strengthening of women-led cooperatives and producer groups.

2. Advance Gender Equality and Leadership

  • Increase women's participation in community governance and peacebuilding platforms.
  • Raise awareness on women's rights and gender-based violence prevention.

3. Strengthen Food Security and Climate Resilience

  • Introduce climate-smart agriculture practices and technologies.
  • Improve access to inputs, irrigation, post-harvest handling, and nutrition education.

4. Foster Peacebuilding and Social Cohesion

  • Engage women and youth in community dialogue and trauma healing initiatives.
  • Support returnee and IDP women with reintegration through livelihood support.

5. Enhance Institutional and Community Capacity

  • Build the capacity of local women-led CBOs and networks.
  • Strengthen CORWADO's internal systems for project management, M&E, and advocacy.
